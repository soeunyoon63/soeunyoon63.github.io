ABOUT WORK

Hold, Please

Brand Identity, Motion

2019

Hold, Please is a campaign about the issue of a long hold for customer service call. I amplified this issue with a set of visual system which consists of a tagline, Hold please, a triadic color scheme, type marks, and audio in loop.

Software Used

Adobe Creative Suite

Cinema4D

PROBLEM

We all have been in the situation where the waiting time to speak to a customer service representative is super long. Accepting the fact that this issue is difficult to be solved in one sitting, I looked at this problem with a neutral stance, suggesting other actives to pass time faster while on hold. A playful element is hinted to encourage the viewers to find ways to make the process go less painfully. I aimed to consider this inevitable problem not merely as negative.

Motion Graphics

On the website, the dashboard has a blank the user can fill out by pressing a key x. The suggested activities that the users can do while waiting for the customer service call are shown in the blank. Below has a story board where people can post about their experiences waiting on hold. The next page is a statistic page, which provides a chart of the number of stories people experience with telecommunications company customer service call. The user can view a diagram of results for the last 30 days once the user hovers over.

Process

The question I wanted to answer while considering the final logo was: “how can I show the longevity the most efficiently?” The solution I came up with was to represent the type marks as both the telephone cord and rhythm of the hold music. I elongated the tagline “hold, please” vertically to mimic how the text looks like when you scroll down on a laggy website.
